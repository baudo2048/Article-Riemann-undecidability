Undecidability, Prime Numbers,
and a Proposed Universal Irrational Constant

Toward a Paradoxical Reformulation of the Riemann Hypothesis

July 2026

Abstract

This paper investigates a speculative relationship between prime numbers, a proposed universal irrational constant, and the logical status of the Riemann Hypothesis. We distinguish established mathematics from conjectural ideas and present a framework for future investigation.

1. Introduction

The Riemann Hypothesis remains one of the greatest open problems in mathematics. This article explores whether the recursive structure of the prime numbers may contain an inherent logical paradox leading to undecidable statements.

2. Background

Definition. A prime number is an integer greater than one having exactly two positive divisors.

Every positive integer factors uniquely into primes.

3. The Riemann Zeta Function

The zeta function is defined by $$ \zeta(s)= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^s}, \qquad \Re(s)>1. $$ Its analytic continuation extends the function to the complex plane except for a simple pole at $s=1$.

Riemann Hypothesis. Every non-trivial zero satisfies $$ \Re(s)=\frac12. $$

4. Universal Irrational Constant

Definition. Assume there exists a constant $$ \Omega $$ generated intrinsically from the recursive structure of the prime numbers.

Its construction and mathematical properties remain to be established.

5. Prime Number Paradox

Suppose the recursive generation of the prime numbers contains a hidden self-referential structure. One may investigate whether this produces an incompressible sequence, algorithmic randomness, or logical independence.

6. Logical Framework

  • Gödel incompleteness
  • Turing computability
  • Formal arithmetic
  • Recursive functions
  • Algorithmic information theory

7. Main Conjecture

Conjecture. If the proposed universal irrational constant uniquely determines the recursive structure of the primes, then the Riemann Hypothesis may be independent of the chosen formal axiomatic system.

8. Open Problems

Construct the proposed universal irrational constant explicitly.
Determine whether the constant is irrational or transcendental.
Determine whether the framework implies undecidability.
Investigate consequences for the Riemann Hypothesis.

9. Conclusion

This article presents a speculative framework relating prime numbers, logical paradox, a universal irrational constant, and the Riemann Hypothesis. Future work will determine whether these ideas can be formalized within accepted mathematical foundations.
